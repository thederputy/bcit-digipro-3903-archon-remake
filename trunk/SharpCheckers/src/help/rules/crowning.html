Crowning the Men

  • When a man first reaches any of the squares on the opposite extreme line of the board, it becomes a King, and must immediately be crowned (by placing a man on top of it) by the opponent. Crowning a piece completes its move - it cannot then capture a piece that is en prise.
  • The King is empowered to move or capture backwards or forwards, as the limits of the board permit. ( The King, like any man, may not refuse to capture when the occasion arises.)
  • If the opponent neglects to crown a man that has reached the King row, and plays on, such play shall be put back until the man be crowned.
